Abstract

[cite_start]Diffusion models demonstrate excellent performance in image generation and synthesis. [cite: 19] [cite_start]Effective and controllable editing with these models requires a deep understanding of their latent spaces, a primary focus of much prior research. [cite: 20] [cite_start]However, existing unsupervised exploration methods like NoiseCLR [5] often remain at attribute-level edits, revealing limitations in complex fine-grained class transformations. [cite: 21] [cite_start]To address this, we propose UDT (Unsupervised Discovery of Transformations), a novel framework that, despite its unsupervised nature, supports fine-grained class transformations by structuring the latent space in a hierarchy-aware manner. [cite: 22] [cite_start]UDT systematically constructs the latent space by incorporating parent class guidance, ensuring semantic consistency across class transformations while preserving pose. [cite: 23] [cite_start]Our hierarchy-informed contrastive learning disentangles class-defining traits, enabling the discovery of diverse and meaningful fine-grained transformation directions. [cite: 24] [cite_start]Experiments on dog, cat, and flower datasets demonstrate that UDT's superior performance over existing methods in generating coherent, diverse, and semantically accurate edits. [cite: 25] [cite_start]These results suggest UDT is a scalable and effective approach for semantic latent space exploration in diffusion models. [cite: 26]
